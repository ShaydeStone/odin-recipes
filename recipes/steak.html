New York Strip Steak

Description

The New York Strip Steak is a classic cut known for its bold beefy flavor and satisfying chew. With a beautiful marbling of fat and a firm texture, it’s the ideal steak for a quick pan-sear and a luxurious dinner at home. This recipe keeps it simple—just salt, pepper, garlic, butter, and a hot skillet.

Ingredients

  • 2 New York strip steaks (about 1–1.5 inches thick)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons high smoke point oil (like avocado or canola)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2–3 sprigs fresh thyme or rosemary (optional)

Steps

  1. Take the steaks out of the fridge 30–45 minutes before cooking. This ensures even cooking.
  2. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels. Season both sides liberally with salt and pepper. Don’t be shy—this is your crust!
  3. Place a heavy skillet (cast iron is best) over high heat. Let it get very hot—about 3–5 minutes.
  4. Add oil to the pan. Carefully place the steaks in the skillet and sear for 3–4 minutes without moving them. Flip and sear the other side for another 3–4 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan. Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steaks repeatedly for about 1–2 minutes.
  6. Check for Doneness. Use a meat thermometer: Rare: 120–125°F, Medium-rare: 130–135°F, Medium: 140–145°F
  7. Transfer to a cutting board and let rest for 5–10 minutes. This helps the juices redistribute.
