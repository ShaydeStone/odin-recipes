Beef Tacos

Description

These beef tacos are juicy, savory, and packed with bold spices. Whether you're feeding a crowd or just craving a taco night, this recipe is quick, customizable, and guaranteed to satisfy. Plus, you can dress them up with all your favorite toppings!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 for best flavor)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: pinch of cayenne for heat
  • 8 small corn or flour tortillas
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • Sour cream or Mexican crema
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Hot sauce or salsa

Steps

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, sauté the chopped onion in a bit of oil until soft (about 3–4 minutes). Add garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  2. Add the ground beef to the skillet. Break it up with a spatula and cook until browned and no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to coat the beef.
  4. Pour in the beef broth or water. Reduce heat and simmer for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and becomes saucy.
  5. While the beef simmers, warm your tortillas in a dry skillet or wrap them in foil and heat in the oven.
  6. Spoon the beef mixture into warm tortillas. Top with lettuce, tomatoes, cheese, sour cream, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
